General Information

The family Felidae includes cats but also lions, tigers, pumas, cheetahs, jaguars, leopards, and more. All felines have round faces with small noses and long tails. Their claws have evolved to help them hunt and tear meat since all felines are carnivorous. Most felines are expert jumpers and climbers, but they are also extremely flexible and can stretch and twist much more than a human can. All felines are mammals and they often carry their young in their mouths. Felines keep their young clean by licking them and babies often rely solely on their mother's milk for 2-4 weeks after birth.

Behavior

Felines often pur when they are content and snarl or growl when they are angry. They will also scratch trees in order to sharpen their claws for hunting.
